Raigad fort -Maharashtra

Raigad Fort is a majestic and historically significant hill fort located in the Raigad district of Maharashtra, India. Perched at an elevation of approximately 2,700 feet above sea level, it is surrounded by the Sahyadri mountain range, making it naturally well-defended and nearly inaccessible from most sides. This fort holds immense historical importance as it was chosen by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj to be the capital of the Maratha Empire. It was here that Shivaji Maharaj was formally crowned as Chhatrapati in 1674, marking the beginning of sovereign Maratha rule in India. Under his guidance, Raigad was transformed from a small hilltop structure (then known as Rairi) into a grand fort with robust walls, watchtowers, and royal buildings. Raigad Fort includes several significant structures such as the Raj Bhavan (King’s Palace), Rani Vasa (Queen’s Quarters), a carefully planned marketplace, and the Raj Sabha (royal court) where Shivaji Maharaj held important meetings. One of the most revered parts of the fort is the Samadhi (tomb) of Shivaji Maharaj, which lies alongside a memorial for his loyal dog.
